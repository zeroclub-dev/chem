Why Surface Tension Is Important

The surface tension of water is so important because it allows dense objects to float that otherwise would not. In general, denser objects sink and less dense objects float, but surface tension provides a barrier that can support some lightweight objects.

Some organisms, like lightweight bugs and water striders, depend on their ability to float to survive, and the surface tension is the reason they can.

Also, the surface tension is high enough that plants can draw the water from the ground, keeping the plants and trees alive. If the water had a weaker surface tension, the plants would not be able to obtain the water they need, meaning they would die.
