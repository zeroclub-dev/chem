Surface Tension Applications

Natural Applications

Surface tension plays a crucial role in many natural processes:

  • Water striders and insects walking on water
  • Capillary action in plants to draw water upward
  • Formation of water droplets and raindrops
  • Transportation of nutrients in plants and trees

Human Applications

We use surface tension in many practical applications:

  • Cleaning and detergents - surfactants reduce surface tension, allowing water to spread and clean more effectively
  • Medical tests that rely on the behavior of fluids
  • Water-repellent surfaces for clothing and electronics
  • Floating objects like paper clips or needles on water for survival techniques
  • Microfluidics in medical and scientific devices
